Community AI Strategy Framework

Designing Your
AI Strategy

A six-step framework for community-focused organisations approaching AI thoughtfully, responsibly, and on their own terms.

01

Start here

Readiness & Impact Assessment

Identify and scope potential use cases — but before selecting an AI-based solution, always ask whether an analogue approach could achieve the same outcome within your timelines and resource constraints. If it can, that should be the default choice. Where AI is the right path, set a clear end goal and concrete review dates.

Use case scoping Analogue-first principle Goal setting
02

Understand the tradeoffs

AI Risk Assessment

Develop a thorough understanding of the risks, limitations, and tradeoffs associated with using AI in each identified use case. This assessment should inform every subsequent decision in the process — it is the foundation everything else rests on.

Risk mapping Limitations analysis Tradeoff review
03

Choose your path

Buy vs. Build vs. Open Source

Assess your solution options carefully. Building in-house keeps ownership and control close to the organisation. Open-source is often equally viable given typical resource constraints. Proprietary, bought-in solutions should generally be the last resort — not the first instinct.

In-house build Open source Procurement
04

Define the detail

Solution Design & Documentation

Finalise the solution design with detailed documentation of all data and model resources being used. Formats such as data cards and model cards are recommended to ensure transparency and accountability are built into the foundation of your deployment.

Data cards Model cards Transparency
05

Build with oversight

Development & Deployment Lifecycle

Design a development and deployment lifecycle with as much human observability built in as feasible — so the system's behaviour can be monitored, questioned, and course-corrected at every stage. Oversight is not a luxury; it is a structural requirement.

Human oversight Monitoring Course-correction
06

Plan the exit

Offboarding & Transition Assessment

At the close of the defined lifecycle, evaluate whether automation achieved its intended goals and plan the appropriate next step — ideally a transition to a community-owned technology solution that places lasting ownership in the hands of the people the organisation was always meant to serve.

Impact review Exit planning Community ownership
